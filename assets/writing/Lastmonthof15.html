Do flowers want to bloom, even if it means humans will pick them? Roses open up on schedule. Sometimes they are not ready to but still do so anyway. It’s a matter of survival; if they don’t start blooming by winter, maybe their kind will have ceased. Maybe growing up is quite like that.

This is my last month being 15. As a kid, I always imagined my teenage years being fun parties, hangouts, and laughing with friends. I’ve always been the ‘good kid’, though I imagined aesthetic rebellions and wild teenage dreams. But now I’m inching towards passing another year of my life with nothing but effort and exhaustion. I had dreams of owning my own company someday and ambitions that I still remember my best friend telling me that I’m reaching too far for. But life happened. Maybe I simply lost interest. Or maybe I stopped doing things because I loved them and started doing them because I was afraid of falling behind.

When I was younger, I thought 16 was old. Only two years before I’m legally an adult. Time flies. And if I’m being honest… I’m terrified. I’m scared of not being ready by then. I thought by then I'd have everything figured out. For 15 years I’ve been in constant fear of being a failure—of being a disappointment to my family. This year, I left home for a scholarship, I finished courses I didn't have to take and built things nobody asked me to build—including a game and my own portfolio website from scratch. While others collected medals and wins like stickers, I collected late nights building projects I worry won’t work. It felt like I was chasing an impossible goal. Like running cross country while the finish line kept moving further away. Like I will spend the rest of my life telling myself that someday I’d be satisfied. Someday I’d enjoy life.

But that someday never came.

I kept telling myself that future me would be grateful. That "I should suffer for ten years then enjoy 60 instead of the other way around," like what all those 17-year-old entrepreneurs on Instagram were telling me. I dream of a day where I’ll be sipping my iced cappuccino, binge-watching shows and simply existing. However, somewhere along the way, I got so focused on building a life in the future...that I forgot to enjoy the life I already had.

My guitar sits in its case in the corner of my room, staring at me with contempt. Stories written halfway in an “Untitled document”. Moments swept by, flowing through my fingers, out of my grasp, while I was busy planning the next best thing. And even now, even after all that work, I still wonder.

Did I do enough at 15?

Did I learn enough?

Did I make the most of it?

Am I ready enough?

At the end of the day the sun continues to set in the west. The moon doesn’t care if they get photographed. The flowers bloom whether they’re ready. After all, maybe “enough” was the wrong question.

Maybe 16 isn't about being ready…maybe nobody ever is.

Maybe it's just another year of learning.

Another year of building.

Another year of becoming.

And maybe that's what life is—learning, especially when you’re not ready.
